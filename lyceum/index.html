Lyceum

Lyceum creates health platforms to support patients suffering from advanced diseases, and to help their healthcare teams monitor their condition and determine their coverage. I joined the company in 2019 as their first designer, and hired another UX Designer in 2021 to help out.

I wore many hats (which I love, but only figuratively): I helped shape the product vision and strategy, coded some of my designs, worked on localization, introduced new tools[notion] and workflows. Oh and yeah, lots of UX!

Digital Therapeutics, early concepts.

Challenges

  • Lot of users. We designed for patients, caregivers, nurses, doctors and pharmaceutical stakeholders.

  • B2B2C model. Patients were hard to reach because of confidentiality issues and their PII (Personally Identifiable Information). Doctors and Nurses had very limited and expensive time.

  • Complex workflows. Each clinic operates differently, so we took the time to understand their differences and how our tools could be best put to use ~

  • No analytics. For privacy and security reasons, we could not use tools such as Google Analytics, Hotjar or Pendo.
